Personal Finance Analysis

A report exploring transaction behavior, spending patterns, and year-to-year changes in category distributions.

Introduction

This project analyzes personal transaction data to better understand spending behavior across time and categories. The goal is to identify meaningful patterns and communicate them through visual and statistical analysis.

Research Question

How do spending habits differ across years (2023-2026) and categories? Are category spending distributions meaningfully different from one year to another?

Data Overview

The dataset contains transaction-level financial records with variables such as: transaction date, amount, category, whether a record is considered credit/debit, and a description of the transaction (similar to a bank memo).

Data Preparation

Steps had to be made to make sure the data was ready for analysis as some info was missing in certain columns

  • Removed columns that were entirely empty
  • There were some transactions that weren't categorized, but it was a small amount so I just manually put them in the correct one

Added some columns to make analysis easier:

  • Using the date column, I extracted the month and year the transaction was made (the time of day however couldn't be extracted due to how Navy Federal stored their data)
  • To analyze spending and income patterns, I defined spending as money leaving my checkings and savings account. A special category that I had to deal with were transactions. I didn't want certain transactions in this category to be marked as either spending or income as it was just money moving between the checkings and savings. However, I did want to mark certain transfers as money being spent and earned, such as money being tranferred to and from external bank accounts.

Key Findings

  • A few categories make up a large portion of total expenses.
  • Some year pairs have noticeably different category distributions.

Conclusion

This analysis shows that transaction data can reveal meaningful behavioral patterns in spending over time. Future work could compare spending with income, study recurring expenses, or model seasonal effects.
